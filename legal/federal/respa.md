# Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA)

## Quick Reference

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act |
| **Citation** | 12 U.S.C. SS 2601-2617 |
| **Enacted** | 1974 |
| **Major Amendments** | 1990 (escrow), 2008 (GFE reform), 2010 (Dodd-Frank), 2015 (TRID) |
| **Implementing Regulation** | Regulation X (12 CFR Part 1024) |
| **Enforcing Agency** | CFPB |

---

## Summary

RESPA was enacted to protect consumers by requiring disclosure of settlement costs, eliminating kickbacks and referral fees that inflate costs, and reducing escrow account overcharges. The Act governs the entire settlement process for federally related mortgage loans, from application through servicing. In 2015, RESPA disclosures were integrated with TILA disclosures through the TRID (TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure) rule, creating the Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure forms.

---

## Scope of Coverage

RESPA applies to "federally related mortgage loans," which includes any loan that:
- Is secured by a first or subordinate lien on residential real property (1-4 family)
- Is made by a lender whose deposits are federally insured, or regulated by a federal agency
- Is intended to be sold to Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, or Ginnie Mae
- Is made by a lender making FHA or VA loans, or is insured/guaranteed by a federal agency

This effectively covers the vast majority of residential mortgage transactions in the United States.

## Key Provisions

### Section 4 & 5: Integrated Disclosures (TRID)
- **Loan Estimate:** Must be provided within 3 business days of receiving a loan application
- **Closing Disclosure:** Must be provided at least 3 business days before consummation
- Replaced the former Good Faith Estimate (GFE) and HUD-1 Settlement Statement
- Standardized format for comparing loan terms and closing costs

### Section 6: Servicing Disclosure and Transfer
- Servicers must provide notice of transfer of servicing at least 15 days before effective date
- Qualified Written Requests (QWRs) must be acknowledged within 5 business days
- Servicers must respond to QWRs within 30 business days
- Protections against fees during dispute resolution period

### Section 8: Kickbacks and Referral Fees
- **Prohibits** giving or accepting any "thing of value" for referral of settlement service business
- **Prohibits** unearned fee splitting (splitting fees when no services are actually performed)
- **Permitted:** Payments for goods or facilities actually furnished, or services actually performed
- **Permitted:** Affiliated business arrangements (AfBAs) with proper disclosure
- **Permitted:** Payments to cooperating brokers, multiple listing services, or referral agents for services actually performed

### Section 9: Title Insurance
- Sellers cannot require buyers to use a particular title insurance company
- Violation is separate offense from Section 8

### Section 10: Escrow Account Limits
- Limits the amount lenders can collect for escrow accounts
- Monthly escrow payments limited to 1/12 of estimated annual taxes and insurance
- Maximum cushion of 2 months of escrow payments
- Annual escrow account analysis required
- Shortages must be addressed; surpluses above $50 must be refunded

### Affiliated Business Arrangements (AfBA)
- Permitted if: (1) written disclosure to consumer, (2) consumer not required to use affiliate, (3) only thing of value received is ownership return on investment
- Disclosure must be provided at or before the time of referral
- Consumer must sign acknowledgment

## Requirements

### For Lenders
- Provide Loan Estimate within 3 business days of application
- Provide Closing Disclosure at least 3 business days before closing
- Maintain escrow accounts within legal limits
- Provide annual escrow account statements
- Properly handle servicing transfers with required notices

### For Settlement Service Providers
- Do not accept or pay referral fees or kickbacks
- Only charge fees for services actually performed
- Disclose affiliated business arrangements
- Provide accurate fee information for disclosures

### For Real Estate Agents and Brokers
- Do not accept referral fees from settlement service providers (unless for services actually performed)
- Disclose affiliated business arrangements
- Do not require use of specific settlement service providers
- Understand the difference between permissible marketing service agreements and prohibited kickbacks

### For Title Companies
- Cannot be required by sellers as a condition of sale
- Cannot pay referral fees to agents/brokers for referrals
- Fees must reflect services actually rendered

## OneMLS Compliance Checklist

### Referral Relationships
- [ ] No referral fee arrangements between OneMLS and settlement service providers
- [ ] Marketing service agreements (MSAs) reviewed for RESPA compliance
- [ ] Any co-marketing arrangements have documented fair market value compensation
- [ ] No tying of MLS services to use of specific settlement providers

- [ ] If OneMLS has ownership interest in settlement services, AfBA disclosures prepared

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Section 8 Violations (Kickbacks/Referral Fees)
| Type | Penalty |
|------|---------|
| Criminal | Up to $10,000 fine and/or 1 year imprisonment per violation |
| Civil (private action) | Up to 3x the settlement service charge |
| Statute of limitations | 1 year from violation (private action) |
| CFPB enforcement | Civil money penalties (tiered) |

### Section 6 Violations (Servicing)
- Actual damages plus additional damages up to $2,000 (individual)
- Class action: actual damages plus up to $1,000,000 or 1% of servicer's net worth
- Attorney fees and costs

### Section 9 Violations (Title Insurance)
- Up to 3x the title insurance charge

### Section 10 Violations (Escrow)
- Excess escrow must be refunded
- Penalties assessed by CFPB

### CFPB Enforcement Actions
- Consent orders with monetary penalties
- Cease and desist orders
- Restitution to affected consumers
- Recent enforcement examples: $1.75 million penalty against mortgage company, $200,000 against brokerage

## Contract Implications

1. **Settlement Cost Estimates:** Loan Estimate must be provided early in the transaction, setting cost expectations
2. **Closing Disclosure Timing:** 3-day waiting period before closing can affect contract timelines
3. **Provider Selection:** Contracts cannot require use of specific settlement service providers (seller cannot mandate title company)
4. **Escrow Provisions:** Contract escrow deposit terms must comply with RESPA escrow limits
5. **Servicing Transfer:** Borrowers have protections when loan servicing is transferred
6. **AfBA Disclosures:** Must be provided when referring to affiliated settlement services

## TRID Integration (Effective October 2015)

The TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure (TRID) rule consolidated mortgage disclosures:

| Old Form | New Form | Timing |
|----------|----------|--------|
| GFE (RESPA) | Loan Estimate | Within 3 business days of application |
| Initial TIL (TILA) | Loan Estimate | Within 3 business days of application |
| HUD-1 (RESPA) | Closing Disclosure | At least 3 business days before closing |
| Final TIL (TILA) | Closing Disclosure | At least 3 business days before closing |

### Changed Circumstances Requiring Revised Loan Estimate
- Changed circumstances affecting settlement costs
- Borrower-requested changes
- Rate lock or expiration of rate lock
- Must be provided within 3 business days of learning of change
