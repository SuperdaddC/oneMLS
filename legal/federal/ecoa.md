# Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)

## Quick Reference

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | Equal Credit Opportunity Act |
| **Citation** | 15 U.S.C. SS 1691-1691f |
| **Enacted** | October 28, 1974 |
| **Major Amendments** | 1976 (expanded protected classes), 2010 (Dodd-Frank appraisal copy requirement), 2021 (CFPB interpretive rule on sexual orientation/gender identity) |
| **Implementing Regulation** | Regulation B (12 CFR Part 1002) |
| **Enforcing Agencies** | CFPB (primary), FTC, DOJ, federal banking regulators |

---

## Summary

The Equal Credit Opportunity Act prohibits discrimination in any aspect of a credit transaction. In the real estate context, ECOA applies to mortgage lending, home equity loans, construction loans, and any other credit secured by real property. The Act covers the entire credit lifecycle from application through servicing and collections. ECOA works in tandem with the Fair Housing Act to prevent lending discrimination, with ECOA focused specifically on credit transactions while the FHA covers broader housing discrimination.

---

## Protected Classes

ECOA prohibits discrimination based on:

1. **Race**
2. **Color**
3. **Religion**
4. **National Origin**
5. **Sex** (including sexual orientation and gender identity per CFPB 2021 interpretive rule)
6. **Marital Status**
7. **Age** (provided the applicant has the capacity to contract)
8. **Receipt of Public Assistance Income** (Social Security, disability, welfare, food stamps, etc.)
9. **Good-Faith Exercise of Rights** under the Consumer Credit Protection Act

## Key Provisions

### Prohibited Practices in Mortgage Lending
- Discouraging applications based on protected class
- Applying different underwriting standards based on protected class
- Offering different terms or conditions (rates, fees, loan amount, down payment)
- Redlining: Refusing to lend in certain neighborhoods based on racial composition
- Requiring a co-signer when the applicant independently qualifies
- Asking about childbearing intentions
- Discounting income from part-time employment, public assistance, alimony, or child support
- Considering immigration status as a basis for denial (when applicant has legal right to work)

### Adverse Action Notices (15 U.S.C. S 1691(d))
When a creditor takes adverse action on a credit application, they must provide:
- Written notice of adverse action
- Statement of specific reasons for the action (or notice of right to request reasons)
- ECOA notice informing applicant of their rights
- Name and address of the federal agency administering compliance
- Must be provided within 30 days of receiving a completed application or 30 days of taking action on an incomplete application

### Appraisal Copy Requirement (Dodd-Frank Addition)
- Creditors must provide applicants a copy of all appraisals and other written valuations
- Applies to first-lien loans secured by a dwelling
- Must be provided promptly upon completion (or 3 business days before consummation, whichever is earlier)
- Applicant may waive the 3-day requirement (must receive copy at or before consummation)
- Applies regardless of whether credit is extended or denied

### Information Collection Rules
- **Cannot ask about:** race, color, religion, national origin, sex of applicant (EXCEPTION: HMDA data collection where required)
- **Cannot ask about:** marital status (except: married, unmarried, separated -- in community property states)
- **Cannot ask about:** plans for having children, birth control practices
- **Can ask about:** age (for scoring systems, to determine applicant has capacity, or to determine special-purpose credit eligibility)
- **Can ask about:** residency/immigration status for purposes of determining eligibility

## Requirements

### For Mortgage Lenders
- Apply uniform underwriting criteria regardless of protected class
- Provide adverse action notices with specific reasons
- Consider all sources of income without discrimination
- Provide appraisal copies for first-lien dwelling loans
- Train loan officers on ECOA requirements
- Monitor lending patterns for disparate impact
- Maintain records of applications and decisions for 25 months (12 months for approved/originated loans)

### For Real Estate Agents/Brokers
- Do not steer clients to or away from lenders based on protected class
- Do not discourage buyers from applying for credit
- Report suspected lending discrimination to appropriate authorities
- Understand that ECOA violations by partner lenders can create legal exposure

### For Appraisers
- Provide objective valuations without regard to protected class of property owner, buyer, or neighborhood demographics
- Do not consider race, color, or national origin of neighborhood residents in valuations
- ECOA and Fair Housing Act both apply to appraisal practices

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Civil Liability (15 U.S.C. S 1691e)
| Type | Maximum Penalty |
|------|----------------|
| Actual damages | Unlimited |
| Punitive damages (individual) | Up to $10,000 |
| Punitive damages (class action) | Lesser of $500,000 or 1% of creditor's net worth |
| Attorney fees and costs | Awarded to prevailing plaintiff |

### Government Enforcement
- CFPB civil money penalties
- DOJ pattern-or-practice lawsuits (no cap on damages)
- Consent decrees with monitoring requirements
- Referral from federal banking regulators to DOJ

### Administrative
- Cease and desist orders
- Restitution programs
- Enhanced monitoring and compliance requirements
- CRA (Community Reinvestment Act) rating impacts

## Contract Implications

1. **Credit Terms:** All terms in real estate financing must be offered without regard to protected class
2. **Adverse Action:** Denied applicants must receive specific reasons, which may affect contract contingency deadlines
3. **Appraisal Copies:** Buyers applying for first-lien loans are entitled to copies of all appraisals used in the decision
4. **Income Consideration:** All legitimate income sources (alimony, child support, public assistance, part-time employment) must be considered if the applicant requests
5. **Co-Signer Requirements:** Cannot require a spouse or specific co-signer based on marital status
6. **Application Processing:** Creditors must process applications in a non-discriminatory manner, affecting contract timeline expectations

## Relationship to Other Laws

| Law | Relationship to ECOA |
|-----|---------------------|
| Fair Housing Act | Overlapping protections; FHA covers broader housing discrimination, ECOA specifically covers credit |
| HMDA | HMDA data used to identify potential ECOA violations |
| TILA | Both regulate lending; TILA focuses on disclosure, ECOA on non-discrimination |
| CRA | Community Reinvestment Act complements ECOA by requiring affirmative lending in underserved areas |
| Dodd-Frank | Enhanced ECOA through appraisal copy requirement and CFPB oversight |
