# Truth in Lending Act (TILA)

## Quick Reference

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | Truth in Lending Act (Title I, Consumer Credit Protection Act) |
| **Citation** | 15 U.S.C. SS 1601-1666j |
| **Enacted** | May 29, 1968 (effective July 1, 1969) |
| **Major Amendments** | 1980 (Simplification), 1994 (HOEPA), 2009 (CARD Act), 2010 (Dodd-Frank), 2013 (TRID) |
| **Implementing Regulation** | Regulation Z (12 CFR Part 1026) |
| **Enforcing Agency** | CFPB |

---

## Summary

The Truth in Lending Act requires creditors to provide standardized disclosures of credit terms so consumers can compare lending options. For real estate transactions, TILA governs mortgage disclosure requirements, the right of rescission for certain transactions, high-cost mortgage protections (HOEPA), advertising rules for credit products, and the ability-to-repay requirement. Since October 2015, TILA and RESPA disclosures have been integrated through the TRID rule.

---

## Key Provisions for Real Estate

### Disclosure Requirements
- **Annual Percentage Rate (APR):** Must be disclosed; represents the true cost of credit
- **Finance Charge:** Total cost of credit expressed as a dollar amount
- **Amount Financed:** Net amount of credit provided
- **Total of Payments:** Sum of all payments over the loan term
- **Payment Schedule:** Number, amount, and due dates of payments
- **Variable Rate Disclosures:** For adjustable-rate mortgages, including index, margin, caps, and worst-case scenario

### Right of Rescission (15 U.S.C. S 1635)
- Applies to non-purchase money mortgages secured by the borrower's principal dwelling
- Covers: refinances, home equity loans, home equity lines of credit (HELOCs)
- Does NOT apply to: purchase money mortgages, construction loans, or subordinate financing for purchase
- **Timeline:** 3 business days after consummation, delivery of material disclosures, or delivery of rescission notice (whichever is latest)
- **Extended Rescission:** Up to 3 years if material disclosures not provided or rescission notice not given
- Upon rescission: security interest is void, creditor must return all fees and charges within 20 days

### HOEPA (Home Ownership and Equity Protection Act)
- Additional protections for "high-cost" mortgages
- Triggers: APR exceeds APOR by 6.5% (first lien) or 8.5% (subordinate lien), OR points/fees exceed 5% of total loan
- Prohibitions: balloon payments, prepayment penalties, due-on-demand clauses, negative amortization
- Requires pre-loan counseling from HUD-approved counselor
- Enhanced disclosure requirements

### Ability to Repay (ATR) and Qualified Mortgage (QM)
- Lenders must make reasonable, good-faith determination of borrower's ability to repay
- Must verify: income/assets, employment, monthly payments on the covered transaction, monthly payments on simultaneous loans, mortgage-related obligations, current debt obligations, monthly DTI ratio, credit history
- **Qualified Mortgage (QM) criteria:** DTI ratio generally not exceeding 43%, no negative amortization, no interest-only payments, no balloon payments (with limited exceptions), points and fees not exceeding 3% of the total loan amount, maximum loan term of 30 years
- QM status provides legal safe harbor or rebuttable presumption of compliance

### Advertising Rules (Regulation Z SS 1026.24)
- **Trigger Terms:** If an advertisement includes any of these, all terms must be disclosed:
  - Down payment amount or percentage
  - Number of payments or period of repayment
  - Amount of any payment
  - Amount of any finance charge
- **Required Disclosures When Triggered:**
  - Amount or percentage of down payment
  - Terms of repayment (including a repayment schedule if rates may increase)
  - APR and whether it may increase
- Advertisements cannot state "no down payment" if a down payment is required
- Misleading rate or payment advertisements are prohibited

## Requirements

### For Lenders/Creditors
- Provide Loan Estimate within 3 business days of application
- Provide Closing Disclosure at least 3 business days before closing
- Make ability-to-repay determination for residential mortgages
- Provide right of rescission notices for applicable transactions
- Comply with HOEPA requirements for high-cost mortgages
- Accurate APR calculation and disclosure

### For Mortgage Brokers
- Provide accurate loan information for disclosures
- Comply with advertising rules
- Do not steer borrowers to higher-cost loans when they qualify for lower-cost options

### For Advertisers (Including Real Estate Platforms)
- Comply with trigger term rules in all mortgage-related advertising
- Display required disclosures when trigger terms are used
- Do not use misleading rate or payment representations

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Civil Liability (15 U.S.C. S 1640)
| Type | Penalty |
|------|---------|
| Individual statutory damages | $200 - $4,000 |
| Class action statutory damages | Lesser of $1,000,000 or 1% of creditor's net worth |
| Actual damages | Unlimited |
| Attorney fees and costs | Awarded to prevailing consumer |

### Right of Rescission
- Entire loan can be voided (borrower returns principal only; creditor returns all fees/charges)
- Extended rescission period (up to 3 years) if disclosures not properly made

### Criminal Penalties (15 U.S.C. S 1611)
- Willful and knowing violations: Up to $5,000 fine and/or 1 year imprisonment

### CFPB Enforcement
- Civil money penalties (tiered by severity)
- Cease and desist orders
- Restitution to consumers
- Consent decrees

## Contract Implications

1. **Loan Estimate Timing:** Application date triggers 3-business-day Loan Estimate requirement, potentially affecting contract timelines
2. **Closing Disclosure Waiting Period:** 3-business-day waiting period before closing; certain changes reset the waiting period (APR increase >0.125%, addition of prepayment penalty, change in loan product)
3. **Right of Rescission:** For refinances and HELOCs, 3-day rescission period after closing; lender cannot disburse funds during this period
4. **HOEPA Counseling:** High-cost mortgage borrowers must receive counseling before closing, adding time to the transaction
5. **ATR/QM:** Lender's ability-to-repay analysis affects loan approval and terms, which directly impact contract contingencies
6. **Seller Financing:** Sellers providing financing are subject to Dodd-Frank/TILA requirements with limited exemptions
