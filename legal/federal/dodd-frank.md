## Summary

The Dodd-Frank Act was enacted in response to the 2008 financial crisis to reform financial regulation and protect consumers. For real estate, the most significant provisions are in Title XIV (Mortgage Reform), which established the ability-to-repay requirement, defined Qualified Mortgages, restricted predatory lending practices, and created the TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure (TRID) framework. The Act also created the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) as the primary federal consumer financial protection agency.

## Title XIV: Mortgage Reform and Anti-Predatory Lending Act

### Ability to Repay (ATR) Requirement

Lenders must make a reasonable, good-faith determination that the borrower has the ability to repay the loan. Required verification factors:

1. Current or reasonably expected income or assets
2. Current employment status
3. Monthly payment on the covered transaction
4. Monthly payment on any simultaneous loan secured by the same property
5. Monthly payment for mortgage-related obligations (taxes, insurance, HOA)
6. Current debt obligations, alimony, and child support
7. Monthly debt-to-income ratio or residual income
8. Credit history

### Qualified Mortgage (QM) Standards

A Qualified Mortgage provides a legal safe harbor (or rebuttable presumption) that the lender complied with ATR requirements.

**QM Criteria:**
- No negative amortization
- No interest-only payments
- No balloon payments (with limited exceptions for small/rural creditors)
- Maximum loan term of 30 years
- Points and fees do not exceed 3% of the total loan amount (thresholds vary for smaller loans)
- Underwriting based on maximum rate during first 5 years
- DTI ratio generally does not exceed 43% (General QM) OR loan is eligible for purchase/guarantee by GSE or federal agency (Temporary QM / GSE Patch -- expired)
- Verification of income, assets, and debts

**QM Categories:**
- **General QM:** Meets all criteria above; uses price-based approach (APR cannot exceed APOR by specified thresholds)
- **Small Creditor QM:** For lenders with assets under $2 billion; allows balloon payments and portfolio-held loans
- **Seasoned QM:** Loans held in portfolio for 36 months with no delinquencies can achieve QM status
- **Government QM:** FHA, VA, USDA, and RHS loans meeting their respective agency requirements

### Prohibited Mortgage Terms and Practices

- **Steering:** Loan originators cannot steer consumers to loans they cannot repay or that have predatory terms
- **Compensation Restrictions:** Loan originators cannot be compensated based on loan terms (except amount of credit extended)
- **Dual Compensation Ban:** Originators cannot receive compensation from both the consumer and another party
- **Mandatory Arbitration Prohibited:** Cannot require arbitration for disputes arising from residential mortgage loans
- **Financing of Credit Insurance:** Cannot finance single-premium credit insurance
- **Prepayment Penalties:** Restricted to QMs, limited to first 3 years, with specific caps

### Appraisal Requirements

- **Higher-Risk Mortgages:** Require written appraisal by certified appraiser with physical property visit
- **Second Appraisal:** Required for higher-risk mortgages when seller acquired property within 180 days at a lower price ("flipping" safeguard)
- **Appraisal Independence:** Prohibits coercion, bribery, or undue influence of appraisers
- **Free Copy:** Borrowers must receive a free copy of any appraisal used in connection with the loan (ECOA requirement, enhanced by Dodd-Frank)

### Mortgage Loan Originator (MLO) Requirements

- All MLOs must be licensed or registered (SAFE Act, Title V of HERA, codified by Dodd-Frank)
- Must pass written exam and background checks
- Continuing education requirements
- Nationwide Mortgage Licensing System (NMLS) registration
- Unique identifier for each MLO

### Seller Financing Restrictions

Dodd-Frank restricts seller financing (since sellers become "loan originators"):

**Exemptions from MLO Requirements:**
- **3-Property Exemption:** Natural persons (not entities) who sell 3 or fewer properties per 12-month period AND
  - Loan is fully amortizing (no balloon)
  - Fixed rate or adjustable after 5+ years with reasonable annual/lifetime caps
  - Seller determines in good faith that buyer can repay
  - Loan meets certain other criteria
- **1-Property Exemption:** Natural persons selling 1 property per 12-month period with fewer restrictions (can include balloon payment)

## Title X: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)

### CFPB Authority Over Real Estate
- Rulemaking authority for RESPA, TILA, ECOA, HMDA, FACTA, and other consumer financial laws
- Supervision and examination of large banks and non-bank financial companies
- Enforcement authority with civil money penalties
- Consumer complaint handling
- Financial literacy and education

### CFPB Civil Money Penalties

| Tier | Violation Type | Maximum Per Day (2025 adjusted) |
|------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| Tier 1 | Any violation of federal consumer financial law | ~$6,703 |
| Tier 2 | Reckless violation | ~$33,517 |
| Tier 3 | Knowing violation | ~$1,340,678 |

## Requirements

### For Mortgage Lenders
- Comply with ATR requirements for all residential mortgage loans
- Meet QM standards to receive safe harbor/rebuttable presumption
- Register all loan originators through NMLS
- Comply with appraisal independence requirements
- Provide integrated TRID disclosures
- Maintain records demonstrating ATR compliance

### For Loan Originators
- Obtain and maintain NMLS registration
- Pass required exams and background checks
- Complete continuing education
- Not steer consumers to unfavorable loan terms
- Comply with compensation restrictions

### For Real Estate Agents/Brokers
- Understand seller financing restrictions when advising clients
- Be aware of MLO licensing requirements for seller-financed transactions
- Ensure lender partners comply with Dodd-Frank requirements
- Advise clients on TRID timing requirements

### For Sellers Providing Financing
- Comply with ATR determination (good-faith assessment of buyer's ability to repay)
- Meet exemption criteria (3-property or 1-property exemption)
- Loan terms must meet specified requirements (amortization, rate type, etc.)
- Consider consulting with attorney on compliance

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### ATR Violations
- Borrower may assert violation as defense to foreclosure at any time during the loan
- Damages include: all finance charges and fees paid, plus actual damages
- Enhanced damages for patterns of violations
- Statute of limitations: 3 years for affirmative claims; no limit for defensive use

### QM Safe Harbor
- QM status provides conclusive presumption of ATR compliance (for non-higher-priced loans)
- Higher-priced QMs receive rebuttable presumption only

### CFPB Enforcement
- See tiered civil money penalties above
- Consent decrees with operational requirements
- Restitution to affected consumers
- Enhanced monitoring

### MLO Violations
- State licensing board sanctions
- Federal penalties for unlicensed activity
- Criminal penalties for fraud in licensing

## Contract Implications

1. **ATR Documentation:** Lenders must document ability-to-repay, affecting mortgage approval timelines
2. **QM Loan Terms:** Most conforming loans will meet QM criteria, limiting available loan structures
3. **Seller Financing:** Contract terms for seller-financed deals must comply with Dodd-Frank restrictions (amortization type, rate caps, balloon limitations)
4. **TRID Timelines:** Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure timing requirements directly affect contract closing dates
5. **Appraisal Requirements:** Higher-risk mortgages may require second appraisal, potentially delaying closing
6. **Prepayment Penalties:** Limited to first 3 years of QMs, affecting refinance provisions
7. **Arbitration Clauses:** Mandatory arbitration clauses in residential mortgage contracts are unenforceable
