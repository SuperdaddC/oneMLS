# Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)

## Quick Reference

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 |
| **Citation** | 42 U.S.C. SS 12101-12213 |
| **Enacted** | July 26, 1990 |
| **Major Amendments** | ADA Amendments Act of 2008 (ADAAA), Pub. L. 110-325 |
| **Implementing Regulations** | 28 CFR Part 35 (Title II), 28 CFR Part 36 (Title III) |
| **Enforcing Agency** | DOJ (primary), EEOC (employment), FCC (telecommunications) |

## Summary

The Americans with Disabilities Act prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in all areas of public life, including employment, education, transportation, and access to public and private places open to the general public. For real estate, the ADA primarily affects commercial properties and public accommodations (real estate offices, brokerage firms, model homes), as well as digital accessibility of real estate platforms and websites. The Fair Housing Act (FHA) is the primary federal law addressing disability discrimination in residential housing, but the ADA supplements it for commercial and public-facing aspects of the real estate industry.

---

## Relevant Titles for Real Estate

### Title II: State and Local Government
- Government-owned or operated facilities must be accessible
- Government housing programs must be accessible
- Public meetings and hearings related to zoning, planning, and development must be accessible
- Government websites and digital services must be accessible

### Title III: Public Accommodations and Commercial Facilities
- Real estate offices, brokerage firms, and property management offices are "public accommodations"
- Model homes open to the public must be accessible
- Leasing offices and sales centers must be accessible
- Commercial properties must be accessible
- Websites and digital platforms of public accommodations must be accessible (per DOJ guidance and case law)

### Title I: Employment (Relevant for Real Estate Firms)
- Real estate firms must provide reasonable accommodations for employees with disabilities
- Cannot discriminate in hiring, firing, or terms of employment based on disability

## Key Requirements

### Physical Accessibility
**New Construction (after January 26, 1993):**
- All new commercial facilities and public accommodations must be accessible
- Must comply with ADA Accessibility Guidelines (ADAAG) / 2010 ADA Standards for Accessible Design
- Accessible entrances, routes, restrooms, parking, and signage

**Existing Facilities:**
- Must remove architectural barriers where "readily achievable" (easily accomplishable without much difficulty or expense)
- Priority areas: entrance access, access to goods/services, restroom access, other measures
- If barrier removal is not readily achievable, must provide alternative methods of access

**Alterations:**
- When renovating existing buildings, altered areas must be made accessible
- Path of travel to altered area must be accessible (up to 20% of alteration cost)

### Digital Accessibility (Websites and Platforms)
- DOJ has consistently taken the position that ADA Title III applies to websites
- April 2024: DOJ published final rule requiring state and local government websites to comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA (Title II)
- Title III web accessibility standards expected to follow similar WCAG requirements
- Multiple court decisions have held websites to be "public accommodations" under Title III

**WCAG 2.1 Level AA Requirements (Key Principles):**
- **Perceivable:** Text alternatives for images, captions for video, sufficient color contrast, resizable text
- **Operable:** Keyboard accessible, sufficient time to interact, no content that causes seizures, navigable structure
- **Understandable:** Readable text, predictable operation, input assistance for forms
- **Robust:** Compatible with assistive technologies (screen readers, voice control, switch devices)

### Reasonable Modifications to Policies
- Must modify policies, practices, and procedures to accommodate individuals with disabilities
- Unless modification would fundamentally alter the nature of the service
- Examples: allowing service animals in offices, providing materials in accessible formats, adjusting appointment scheduling

### Effective Communication
- Must provide auxiliary aids and services to ensure effective communication
- Examples: sign language interpreters, large print documents, accessible electronic formats
- Must give primary consideration to the person's preferred method of communication

## ADA vs. Fair Housing Act for Disability

| Aspect | ADA | Fair Housing Act |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| **Residential Housing** | Generally does not cover private residential housing | Primary law for residential disability discrimination |
| **Commercial/Public Accommodation** | Covers offices, sales centers, model homes | Does not cover commercial spaces |
| **Website/Digital** | Covers digital platforms of public accommodations | Does not specifically address digital accessibility |
| **Design & Construction** | Commercial and public facilities | Multifamily dwellings (4+ units) built after March 1991 |
| **Reasonable Accommodation** | Required for public accommodations | Required for housing providers |
| **Reasonable Modification** | Must allow physical modifications to facilities | Must allow tenants to modify at their expense |
| **Enforcement** | DOJ, private lawsuits | HUD, DOJ, private lawsuits |

## Requirements

### For Real Estate Brokerages and Offices
- Physical office must be accessible (or barriers removed where readily achievable)
- Website and online tools must be accessible
- Marketing materials available in accessible formats
- Service animals must be permitted
- Effective communication with clients who have hearing, vision, or cognitive disabilities
- Reasonable modifications to standard practices for clients with disabilities

### For MLS Organizations
- MLS platform must be accessible to members with disabilities
- Data feeds and IDX implementations should support accessible display
- Training and educational materials must be accessible
- Meetings and events must be accessible
- Digital tools and mobile applications must be accessible

### For Property Managers (Commercial)
- Commercial leased spaces must meet ADA accessibility requirements
- Common areas must be accessible
- Tenant buildouts must comply with ADA for new construction/alterations
- Emergency evacuation plans must accommodate individuals with disabilities

### For Developers and Builders
- New commercial construction must meet ADA Standards for Accessible Design
- Model homes open to public must be accessible
- Sales centers and leasing offices must be accessible
- Parking areas must include accessible spaces

## OneMLS Compliance Checklist

### Web Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA)
- [ ] All images have descriptive alt text (including property photos)
- [ ] Videos have captions and audio descriptions
- [ ] Color contrast ratios meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
- [ ] All functionality accessible via keyboard navigation
- [ ] Focus indicators visible for keyboard users
- [ ] Skip navigation links provided
- [ ] Form fields have proper labels and error messages
- [ ] ARIA landmarks and roles used appropriately
- [ ] Page structure uses proper heading hierarchy (h1-h6)
- [ ] Dynamic content updates announced to screen readers
- [ ] No content that flashes more than 3 times per second
- [ ] Text can be resized up to 200% without loss of content
- [ ] Consistent navigation across all pages
- [ ] Error identification and suggestion for form inputs
- [ ] Time limits can be adjusted or extended

### Mobile Application Accessibility
- [ ] Touch targets at least 44x44 CSS pixels
- [ ] Compatible with platform accessibility features (VoiceOver, TalkBack)
- [ ] Orientation not restricted (supports portrait and landscape)
- [ ] Gestures have alternative input methods
- [ ] Status messages communicated without focus change

### Document Accessibility
- [ ] PDF documents are tagged and accessible
- [ ] Contract templates are accessible to screen readers
- [ ] Disclosure forms available in accessible formats
- [ ] Email communications are accessible (HTML with proper structure)

### Property Listing Accessibility
- [ ] Property photos have meaningful alt text
- [ ] Virtual tours have accessible alternatives
- [ ] Map/location features have text-based alternatives
- [ ] Listing search results navigable by keyboard
- [ ] Filter controls accessible to assistive technology
- [ ] Property detail pages have proper structure and headings

### Testing and Monitoring
- [ ] Regular automated accessibility scans (axe, WAVE, Lighthouse)
- [ ] Manual testing with screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
- [ ] Keyboard-only navigation testing
- [ ] User testing with people who have disabilities
- [ ] Accessibility statement published on platform
- [ ] Process for receiving and addressing accessibility complaints
- [ ] Third-party accessibility audit conducted annually

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Civil Penalties (DOJ Enforcement)
| Offense | Maximum Penalty |
|---------|----------------|
| First violation | Up to $75,000 |
| Subsequent violations | Up to $150,000 |

### Private Lawsuits
- Injunctive relief (court orders to fix accessibility issues)
- Attorney fees and costs (plaintiff's attorneys)
- No compensatory or punitive damages under federal ADA (but available under many state laws)
- Consent decrees with monitoring

### State Law Penalties
- Many states have additional accessibility laws with private damages
- California Unruh Civil Rights Act: Minimum $4,000 per violation plus actual damages
- New York: Additional state remedies
- Other states may have parallel or more protective laws

### Business Impact
- Website accessibility lawsuits have increased dramatically (11,000+ in 2023)
- Real estate industry is a frequent target for accessibility lawsuits
- Remediation costs increase when forced by litigation vs. proactive compliance
- Reputational damage

## Contract Implications

1. **Commercial Leases:** Must allocate ADA compliance responsibility between landlord and tenant
2. **Property Condition:** ADA compliance status should be part of commercial property due diligence
3. **Construction Contracts:** Must include ADA compliance requirements for new construction and renovations
4. **Service Agreements:** Contracts with technology vendors should require WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
5. **Reasonable Accommodation:** Real estate service contracts should acknowledge obligation to provide reasonable modifications
6. **Insurance:** ADA compliance requirements should be addressed in liability insurance provisions
7. **Indemnification:** Commercial leases often include ADA indemnification provisions

---

## Key Court Decisions Relevant to Digital Accessibility

- **Robles v. Domino's Pizza (9th Cir. 2019):** Websites and apps of public accommodations must be accessible under ADA
- **Gil v. Winn-Dixie (11th Cir. 2021):** Website-only claims may not support standalone ADA claims (circuit split)
- **NAD v. Netflix (D. Mass. 2012):** Streaming services must provide captions under ADA
- **DOJ Final Rule (April 2024):** State/local government websites must comply with WCAG 2.1 AA (Title II)
