# Fair Housing Act (FHA)

## Quick Reference

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | Fair Housing Act (Title VIII of the Civil Rights Act of 1968) |
| **Citation** | 42 U.S.C. SS 3601-3619 |
| **Enacted** | April 11, 1968 |
| **Major Amendments** | Fair Housing Amendments Act of 1988 (Pub. L. 100-430) |
| **Implementing Regulation** | 24 CFR Part 100 |
| **Enforcing Agencies** | HUD (primary), DOJ (pattern/practice cases) |

---

## Summary

The Fair Housing Act is the primary federal law prohibiting discrimination in housing. It declares that it is the policy of the United States to provide fair housing throughout the country. The Act makes it unlawful to discriminate in the sale, rental, advertising, or financing of housing on the basis of seven protected classes. The 1988 amendments added familial status and disability as protected classes and strengthened enforcement mechanisms.

---

## Protected Classes

1. **Race**
2. **Color**
3. **National Origin**
4. **Religion**
5. **Sex** (includes sexual orientation and gender identity per HUD 2021 guidance and Executive Order 13988)
6. **Familial Status** (families with children under 18, pregnant women, people securing custody of children)
7. **Disability** (physical or mental handicap)

---

## Prohibited Practices

### In Sales and Rentals (42 U.S.C. S 3604)
- Refusing to sell or rent based on protected class
- Discriminating in terms, conditions, or privileges of sale or rental
- Making statements indicating discriminatory preference or limitation
- Representing a property as unavailable when it is available (to a protected class)
- Blockbusting (inducing sales by representing that persons of a protected class are entering the neighborhood)
- Denying access to or membership in MLS or real estate organizations

### In Lending (42 U.S.C. S 3605)
- Discriminating in making loans for purchasing, constructing, improving, repairing, or maintaining a dwelling
- Discriminating in terms/conditions of financing (interest rate, points, fees)

### In Brokerage Services (42 U.S.C. S 3606)
- Denying access to MLS membership or services based on protected class
- Discriminating in terms or conditions of membership in any MLS, broker organization, or related service

### In Advertising (42 U.S.C. S 3604(c))
- Publishing advertisements that indicate discriminatory preferences, limitations, or discrimination
- Applies to all media: print, online, MLS listing descriptions, social media

### Disability-Specific Requirements (42 U.S.C. S 3604(f))
- Reasonable modifications: Landlords must allow tenants with disabilities to make reasonable modifications at tenant expense
- Reasonable accommodations: Must make reasonable accommodations in rules, policies, or services
- Design and construction: Multifamily dwellings (4+ units) built after March 1991 must meet accessibility requirements

## Exemptions

The FHA has limited exemptions:

1. **Owner-Occupied Small Housing:** Sale or rental of a single-family home by the owner (without a broker) if the owner owns no more than 3 single-family homes
2. **Mrs. Murphy Exemption:** Owner-occupied buildings with 4 or fewer units
3. **Religious Organizations:** May limit sale, rental, or occupancy to members of the same religion (if membership is not restricted by race, color, or national origin)
4. **Private Clubs:** May limit rental or occupancy to members for non-commercial purposes
5. **Housing for Older Persons:** Exempt from familial status requirements if qualifying under 42 U.S.C. S 3607(b)

**Important:** Exemptions do NOT apply to discriminatory advertising. All advertising must comply with the FHA regardless of exemption status.

## Requirements

### For Real Estate Agents and Brokers
- Treat all clients equally regardless of protected class
- Use non-discriminatory language in all listings and marketing
- Do not steer buyers toward or away from neighborhoods
- Do not provide different levels of service based on protected class
- Display Equal Housing Opportunity logo and statement
- Complete fair housing training

### For MLS Organizations
- Open membership to all qualified real estate professionals regardless of protected class
- Ensure listing data and search functionality do not facilitate discrimination
- Enforce fair housing language standards in listing descriptions
- Report discriminatory listings or practices

### For Lenders
- Evaluate all loan applications using consistent, non-discriminatory criteria
- Do not engage in redlining (denying services to specific neighborhoods based on racial composition)
- Provide equal terms and conditions to all qualified applicants

### For Sellers and Landlords
- Make housing available on equal terms
- Comply with reasonable accommodation/modification requests (disability)
- Do not set discriminatory occupancy standards

## OneMLS Compliance Checklist

### Listing Content
- [ ] Implement automated listing language review for discriminatory terms
- [ ] Maintain a prohibited words/phrases list based on HUD and NAR guidelines
- [ ] Flag listings with potentially discriminatory language for review
- [ ] Require fair housing acknowledgment from listing agents

### Search and Display
- [ ] Search filters must not enable filtering by race, religion, or national origin of neighborhoods
- [ ] Do not display demographic data that could facilitate steering
- [ ] Ensure school district data (if shown) does not serve as proxy for racial composition
- [ ] Walk Score and similar metrics must be applied uniformly

### Platform Access
- [ ] MLS membership open to all qualified professionals regardless of protected class
- [ ] Equal access to all platform features, data, and services
- [ ] Non-discriminatory fee structures
- [ ] Accessible membership application process

### Advertising
- [ ] All platform-generated marketing complies with fair housing advertising guidelines
- [ ] Agent marketing tools enforce fair housing language standards
- [ ] Equal Housing Opportunity logo displayed on platform
- [ ] Social media sharing tools do not enable discriminatory targeting

### Training and Enforcement
- [ ] Fair housing training requirements for all platform users
- [ ] Complaint mechanism for fair housing violations
- [ ] Investigation and enforcement procedures
- [ ] Regular fair housing audits of listing content

### Data and Analytics
- [ ] Analytics tools do not enable discriminatory analysis
- [ ] CMA (Comparative Market Analysis) tools do not incorporate protected class data
- [ ] Algorithmic fairness review for any AI/ML features
- [ ] Avoid disparate impact in platform algorithms

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Administrative (HUD ALJ)
| Offense | Maximum Penalty (2025) |
|---------|----------------------|
| First violation | $26,262 |
| Second (within 5 years) | $65,653 |
| Third or more (within 7 years) | $131,308 |

### Federal Court (DOJ or Private Action)
- Unlimited compensatory damages
- Unlimited punitive damages
- Attorney fees and costs
- Injunctive relief
- Consent decrees with monitoring

### Additional Consequences
- State and local fair housing penalties (often additional)
- MLS/association disciplinary action
- License revocation or suspension
- Reputational damage
- Loss of business relationships

---

## Contract Implications

1. **Equal Terms:** All contracts must be offered on equal terms regardless of buyer/seller protected class
2. **Non-Discrimination Clauses:** Standard contract forms should include fair housing compliance provisions
3. **Advertising:** Listing agreements should require fair housing compliant advertising
4. **Reasonable Accommodations:** Contract terms should accommodate disability-related needs
5. **Anti-Steering:** Buyer representation agreements should not limit geographic areas in a discriminatory manner
6. **MLS Rules:** MLS participation agreements should incorporate fair housing requirements

---

## Key Court Decisions

- **Texas Department of Housing v. Inclusive Communities (2015):** Disparate impact claims are cognizable under the FHA
- **Trafficante v. Metropolitan Life Insurance (1972):** Standing extends to all residents, not just direct targets of discrimination
- **National Fair Housing Alliance v. Facebook (2019 settlement):** Fair housing applies to digital advertising targeting
