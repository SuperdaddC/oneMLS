# Mello-Roos Special Tax District Disclosure

> **Primary Authority:** California Civil Code Section 1102.6b; Government Code Sections 53311-53368 (Mello-Roos Community Facilities Act of 1982)
> **Related Statutes:** Government Code 53340.2 (Notice of Special Tax)
> **Disclosure Form:** Notice of Special Tax (NST)

---

## Overview

The Mello-Roos Community Facilities Act of 1982 (Government Code 53311 et seq.) allows cities, counties, and special districts to create Community Facilities Districts (CFDs) to finance public improvements and services. Properties within these districts are subject to special tax assessments that are separate from and in addition to standard property taxes.

Sellers must disclose the existence and terms of Mello-Roos assessments to prospective buyers.

---

## What is a Mello-Roos District?

### Purpose
CFDs may be established to finance:
- Public infrastructure (roads, water, sewer, drainage)
- Schools and educational facilities
- Libraries and parks
- Fire and police facilities
- Utility infrastructure

### How It Works
- A CFD is formed by a 2/3 vote of property owners (or registered voters if fewer than 12 registered voters reside in the proposed district)
- The CFD issues bonds to fund improvements
- The bonds are repaid through annual special tax assessments on properties within the district
- The special tax appears as a line item on the property tax bill
- Mello-Roos taxes are NOT based on property value -- they are a flat tax or formula-based assessment

---

## Disclosure Requirements

### Seller's Duty (Civil Code 1102.6b)

A seller whose property is subject to a Mello-Roos assessment must make a **good faith effort** to:
1. Obtain the **Notice of Special Tax** (defined in Government Code 53340.2) from the CFD
2. Deliver the Notice of Special Tax to the prospective buyer

### Contents of Notice of Special Tax (Government Code 53340.2)
The Notice of Special Tax must include:
- Description of the Community Facilities District
- The services or facilities being financed
- The current annual special tax amount
- The **maximum special tax** amount that may be levied
- The date when the special tax terminates (bond maturity)
- Information about how the special tax may change over time

### New Home Sales vs. Resales

**New Construction (Subdivisions):**
- Government Code 11010 requires the Public Report ("White Paper") to disclose all indebtedness that is a lien on the subdivision
- Mello-Roos must be disclosed in the Public Report
- Builder must provide Notice of Special Tax at or before close of escrow

**Resales:**
- No Public Report requirement
- Seller must make good faith effort to obtain and provide the Notice of Special Tax
- Agent may need to research the existence and terms of Mello-Roos bonds
- The tax information may be found on the property tax bill, from the county tax collector, or from the CFD directly

---

## When Disclosure Must Be Made

- **Before transfer of title** (as part of the overall disclosure package)
- The Notice of Special Tax should be delivered along with other disclosures (TDS, NHD, etc.)
- Rescission rights apply if delivered after offer execution (3 or 5 days per standard rules)

---

## Who Must Comply

| Party | Obligation |
|---|---|
| Seller | Good faith effort to obtain and deliver Notice of Special Tax |
| Listing Agent | Ensure seller's disclosure obligations are met; disclose actual knowledge of Mello-Roos |
| Buyer's Agent | Advise buyer to investigate property tax bill for special assessments |
| CFD/Issuing Agency | Provide Notice of Special Tax upon request |

---

## How to Identify Mello-Roos Assessments

### Methods for Discovery
1. **Property tax bill** -- Mello-Roos assessments appear as separate line items
2. **County tax collector records** -- Available online for most California counties
3. **Title report/preliminary title report** -- May show CFD liens
4. **NHD report** -- Many third-party NHD companies include Mello-Roos information
5. **Direct inquiry to the CFD** -- Contact the issuing agency

### Common Indicators
- Line items on tax bill labeled "CFD" or "Community Facilities District"
- Reference to specific bond series or improvement area numbers
- Higher-than-expected property tax bills relative to assessed value

---

## Impact on Property and Transaction

### Financial Impact
- Mello-Roos taxes can add **hundreds to thousands of dollars** annually to the property tax bill
- The tax is a **senior lien** on the property (same priority as regular property taxes)
- Failure to pay Mello-Roos taxes can result in tax sale of the property
- The assessment typically runs for **25-40 years** (until bonds are retired)

### Mortgage Qualification Impact
- Lenders include Mello-Roos taxes in the buyer's debt-to-income ratio calculations
- High Mello-Roos assessments may reduce the buyer's borrowing capacity
- Some lenders require Mello-Roos to be impounded with the loan payment

### Transferability
- Mello-Roos assessments run with the land -- they transfer with the property
- The buyer assumes all future Mello-Roos tax obligations upon taking title
- The assessment cannot be prepaid in most cases (depends on CFD terms)

---

## Penalties for Non-Disclosure

- **Actual damages** for failure to disclose (same framework as TDS violations)
- No specific statutory penalty beyond actual damages
- Agent may face DRE disciplinary action for failing to ensure proper disclosure
- Buyer may have grounds for rescission if Mello-Roos was not disclosed and materially affects the transaction

---

## Interaction with Other Disclosures

- Mello-Roos information is often included in third-party NHD/tax disclosure reports
- The TDS asks about "any special tax assessment" but the specific Notice of Special Tax is a separate document
- Supplemental Property Tax Disclosure (Civil Code 1102.6c) is a separate requirement

---

## Contract Builder Implementation Notes

### Detection Logic
1. Query property tax records for CFD/Mello-Roos line items
2. Check title report for CFD liens
3. Cross-reference property address with known CFD boundaries
4. Flag properties in areas with common Mello-Roos assessments (master-planned communities, newer subdivisions)

### Required Data
- CFD name and number
- Annual special tax amount (current)
- Maximum special tax amount
- Bond maturity date / tax termination date
- Issuing agency contact information

### Validation Rules
- If Mello-Roos is identified, Notice of Special Tax must be included in disclosure package
- Track delivery date for rescission window
- Flag if Notice of Special Tax could not be obtained (document good faith effort)
