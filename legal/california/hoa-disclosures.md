# HOA / Common Interest Development (CID) Disclosures

> **Primary Authority:** California Civil Code Sections 4525 - 4530 (Davis-Stirling Common Interest Development Act)
> **Related Statutes:** Civil Code 4000-6150 (Davis-Stirling Act generally)
> **C.A.R. Forms:** HOA-IR (HOA Information Request); HOA documents package

---

## Overview

When a property is part of a Common Interest Development (CID) -- such as a condominium, townhome, planned unit development (PUD), or cooperative -- the seller must provide the buyer with extensive HOA-related documents and disclosures. The Davis-Stirling Common Interest Development Act governs these requirements.

---

## Types of Common Interest Developments (Civil Code 4100)

1. **Condominium** -- individual units with shared common areas
2. **Planned Development (PUD)** -- individually owned lots with shared common areas
3. **Stock Cooperative** -- ownership of stock in a corporation that owns the development
4. **Community Apartment Project** -- undivided interest in land with exclusive occupancy rights

---

## Documents Required to Be Provided to Buyer (Civil Code 4525)

The owner (seller) must provide the following to a prospective buyer **as soon as practicable before transfer of title or execution of a real property sales contract:**

### Governing Documents
1. **CC&Rs** (Declaration of Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions)
2. **Articles of Incorporation** of the association
3. **Bylaws** of the association
4. **Operating Rules** (rules and regulations adopted by the board)

### Financial Documents
5. **Annual budget report** (most recent)
   - Includes pro forma operating budget
   - Summary of association's reserves
   - Statement of deferred maintenance
6. **Assessment and reserve funding disclosure summary**
   - Required by Civil Code 5300
   - Includes current regular and special assessments
   - Reserve funding plan and percent funded
7. **Most recent financial statement** (reviewed or audited if available)
8. **Written statement of any outstanding assessments** owed by the selling owner

### Insurance
9. **Summary of association's insurance coverage** (or copy of insurance certificate)

### Litigation and Claims
10. **Written statement from association** regarding:
    - Any pending litigation involving the association
    - Any pending or anticipated special assessments
    - Any known claims or conditions that may require special assessment
    - Any current or anticipated lawsuits

### Condition and Maintenance
11. **Minutes of board meetings** (for the prior 12 months)
12. **Any available inspection reports** for the property or common areas

### Age-Restricted Communities
13. If applicable, statement regarding **age restrictions** (e.g., 55+ communities under Civil Code 51.3)

## Document Disclosure Form (Civil Code 4528)

### Form Requirements
- The documents must be accompanied by a form itemizing what is being provided
- The form must be in **at least 10-point type**
- The form must list each required document and indicate whether it is included
- This is commonly called the "4528 Form"

### Fee Disclosure
The form must also disclose:
- What fees were charged for document preparation/delivery
- The legal basis for each fee
- Whether additional fees may be charged by the association

---

## Association's Obligation to Provide Documents (Civil Code 4530)

### Timeline
- Upon **written request** from the owner (seller), the association must provide the required documents **within 10 days** of mailing or delivery of the request
- The association cannot withhold documents for any reason other than non-payment of the document fee

### Fees
- The association may charge a **reasonable fee** based on actual cost for:
  - Procurement of documents
  - Preparation of documents
  - Reproduction of documents
  - Delivery of documents
- **Electronic delivery:** No additional fees may be charged for electronic delivery
- Rush fees may apply for expedited requests

### Prohibition on Withholding
- Documents **cannot be withheld** as leverage or for reasons unrelated to fee payment
- The association cannot condition delivery on payment of delinquent assessments (only document fees)

---

## Buyer's Rescission Rights

### Right to Cancel After Receiving HOA Documents
If the HOA documents are delivered **after** the buyer has signed the purchase agreement, the buyer has the right to rescind:

| Delivery Method | Rescission Period |
|---|---|
| In person | 3 calendar days |
| By mail | 5 calendar days |
| Electronic delivery | 5 calendar days |

### How to Exercise Rescission
- Buyer must deliver written notice of rescission to seller or seller's agent
- Rescission right expires at close of escrow if not exercised within the prescribed period

---

## Seller's Responsibilities

### What the Seller Must Do
1. **Request HOA documents** from the association (typically via HOA-IR form)
2. **Pay document fees** charged by the association (who ultimately bears cost may be negotiated)
3. **Deliver documents to buyer** as soon as practicable before close
4. **Disclose known HOA issues** on the TDS and SPQ, including:
   - Pending or anticipated special assessments
   - Construction defect litigation
   - CC&R violations or disputes
   - Neighbor complaints or disputes involving the HOA
   - Planned HOA rule changes that may affect the property

### What the Seller Need Not Do
- Seller is not required to obtain documents that don't exist
- Seller is not required to verify the accuracy of association-provided documents
- Seller may rely on documents provided by the association in good faith

---

## Agent's Responsibilities

### Listing Agent
- Ensure seller requests HOA documents promptly after listing
- Track document status and delivery
- Advise seller of disclosure obligations related to HOA
- Review documents for obvious red flags

### Buyer's Agent
- Advise buyer to review HOA documents carefully before removing contingencies
- Point out significant items: reserve funding levels, pending assessments, litigation
- Recommend professional review of CC&Rs if complex restrictions exist
- Ensure buyer has adequate time to review before contingency removal

---

## Key Items Buyers Should Review

### Financial Health Indicators
- **Reserve fund percentage funded** -- below 30% is concerning; below 15% is a red flag
- **Pending or planned special assessments** -- can be thousands of dollars per unit
- **Delinquent assessments** from other owners -- can affect association's financial stability
- **Monthly assessment amount** -- impacts buyer's debt-to-income ratio

### Legal Exposure
- **Active litigation** -- construction defect suits, personal injury, contract disputes
- **Insurance claims** -- history of claims may indicate underlying problems

### Lifestyle Restrictions
- **Rental restrictions** -- may limit buyer's ability to rent the unit
- **Pet restrictions** -- breed, size, or number limits
- **Parking rules** -- assigned spaces, guest parking
- **Architectural controls** -- restrictions on modifications
- **Short-term rental prohibition** -- Airbnb/VRBO restrictions

---

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Party | Violation | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Seller | Failure to deliver HOA documents | Buyer may rescind; actual damages |
| Association | Failure to provide within 10 days | Liability for resulting damages; violation of Davis-Stirling Act |
| Agent | Failure to ensure HOA disclosure | DRE discipline; actual damages |

---

## Special Situations

### New Construction Condos
- Developer must provide Public Report (DRE White Paper)
- CC&Rs must be recorded before sale
- Budget, reserve study, and insurance must be established
- Developer retains control until turnover to homeowner-elected board

### Conversion Projects (Apartments to Condos)
- Additional disclosure requirements under Government Code 66427.1
- Physical inspection report required
- Notice to existing tenants required

### HOA Transfer Fee Disclosure
- Some HOAs charge transfer fees when a unit is sold
- These fees must be disclosed to the buyer
- Capital improvement or enhancement fees must be separately identified

## Contract Builder Implementation Notes

### HOA Detection
1. Check property records for CID/HOA association
2. Query title records for CC&R references
3. Flag condominium, townhome, and PUD property types

### Required Data
- HOA name and management company
- Monthly assessment amount
- Special assessments (current and pending)
- Reserve fund percentage
- Key CC&R restrictions
- Pending litigation summary
- Insurance coverage summary

### Workflow
1. Generate HOA document request (HOA-IR) at listing
2. Track 10-day response deadline from association
3. Include HOA documents in buyer disclosure package
4. Track buyer's review period and rescission window
5. Confirm all 4525 documents received before contingency removal

### Validation Rules
- If property is a CID: all 4525 documents must be delivered before close
- Track document delivery date for rescission calculation
- Flag if reserve funding is below 30%
- Flag pending litigation or special assessments
- Verify HOA document fees are disclosed per 4528
