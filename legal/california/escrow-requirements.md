# Escrow and Closing Requirements

> **Primary Authority:** California Financial Code Sections 17000 - 17700 (Escrow Law)
> **Regulatory Agency:** Department of Financial Protection and Innovation (DFPI)
> **Related Statutes:** BPC 10141.6 (broker escrow exemption); Insurance Code (title insurance); Revenue and Taxation Code (transfer tax)
> **Federal:** RESPA (12 USC 2601 et seq.); FIRPTA (IRC 1445)

---

## Overview

California is an "escrow state" -- real estate transactions close through a neutral third-party escrow holder rather than through an attorney closing (common in East Coast states). The Escrow Law regulates independent escrow companies, while certain other entities are exempt from licensing.

---

## What is Escrow?

### Definition (Financial Code 17003)
An escrow is any transaction in which one person, for the purpose of effecting the sale, transfer, encumbering, or leasing of real or personal property to another person, delivers any written instrument, money, evidence of title to real or personal property, or other thing of value to a third person to be held by that third person until the happening of a specified event or the performance of a prescribed condition.

### Key Principles
- **Neutral third party** -- the escrow holder does not represent either party
- **Conditional delivery** -- funds and documents are held until all conditions are met
- **Dual instructions** -- escrow follows written instructions from both buyer and seller
- **Fiduciary duty** -- escrow holder owes fiduciary duties to both parties

---

## Who Can Serve as Escrow Holder

### Licensed Escrow Companies
- Must be licensed by the DFPI under Financial Code 17200
- Must be organized as a California corporation
- Must maintain minimum financial requirements:
  - Liquid assets exceeding current liabilities by $25,000
  - Tangible assets exceeding total liabilities by $50,000
- Must maintain fidelity bond
- Subject to annual audits

### Exempt Entities (Financial Code 17006)
The following may provide escrow services WITHOUT an escrow license:
1. **Licensed real estate brokers** -- when performing escrow incidental to a real estate transaction in which they are the agent (BPC 10141.6)
2. **Banks and savings institutions** -- regulated under Banking Law
3. **Title insurance companies** -- regulated under Insurance Code
4. **Attorneys** -- actively practicing law in California
5. **Licensed securities dealers**

### In Practice
Most residential escrow in California is handled by:
- Title insurance companies (who also provide title insurance)
- Independent escrow companies
- Occasionally, real estate brokers (smaller transactions)

## The Escrow Process

### 1. Opening Escrow
- Purchase agreement is executed and delivered to escrow
- Escrow officer prepares escrow instructions based on purchase agreement terms
- Escrow number is assigned
- Earnest money deposit is collected and deposited into escrow trust account

### 2. Escrow Instructions
- Written instructions from buyer and seller
- May be a single bilateral set or separate unilateral instructions from each party
- Must include:
  - Purchase price and terms
  - Allocation of closing costs
  - Prorations
  - Conditions for closing (loan approval, inspections, disclosures)
  - Disbursement instructions
  - Recording instructions

### 3. During Escrow
- Title search and preliminary title report issued
- Buyer conducts inspections and reviews disclosures
- Buyer applies for and obtains loan approval
- Appraisal is conducted (if financed)
- Contingencies are removed
- Buyer deposits remaining down payment and closing costs
- Seller signs deed and other transfer documents
- Lender funds the loan

### 4. Closing Escrow
- All conditions in escrow instructions have been met
- Escrow officer verifies:
  - All documents are signed and notarized as required
  - All funds have been deposited
  - All contingencies removed
  - Title is clear (or insurable with known exceptions)
- Grant deed and deed of trust are recorded with the county recorder
- Funds are disbursed per instructions
- Closing statements prepared and delivered to both parties

### 5. Post-Closing
- Title insurance policy issued
- Recording confirmation sent to parties
- Escrow file retained per record-keeping requirements

## Key Escrow Documents

### From Seller
1. **Grant deed** -- conveys title from seller to buyer
2. **Affidavit of non-foreign status** (FIRPTA) -- certifies seller is not a foreign person
3. **California Form 593** -- California tax withholding certificate
4. **Payoff statements** -- for existing loans
5. **HOA documents** -- estoppel certificate, if applicable
6. **All required disclosures** -- TDS, NHD, lead paint, etc.

### From Buyer
1. **Loan documents** -- promissory note, deed of trust (if financed)
2. **Down payment and closing costs** -- typically wired to escrow
3. **Proof of insurance** -- homeowner's insurance policy
4. **Contingency removal forms** -- written removal of all contingencies
5. **Vesting instructions** -- how buyer will take title

### From Escrow
1. **Escrow instructions** -- bilateral or unilateral
2. **Preliminary closing statement** -- estimated costs and prorations
3. **Final closing statement (settlement statement)** -- actual amounts
4. **Recording instructions** -- to county recorder

## Prorations and Adjustments

### Items Typically Prorated at Closing

| Item | Proration Method |
|---|---|
| Property taxes | Based on tax year; seller pays through close, buyer pays from close forward |
| HOA dues | Monthly; prorated based on close date |
| Rents (income property) | Monthly; assigned to party who owns on collection date |
| Interest on existing loans | Daily; prorated through payoff date |
| Prepaid items | Credits for unused prepaid amounts |
| Utility bills | May be prorated or handled by utility company |

### Property Tax Proration
- California property taxes are paid in two installments:
  - 1st installment: November 1 (due), December 10 (delinquent)
  - 2nd installment: February 1 (due), April 10 (delinquent)
- Tax year runs July 1 to June 30
- Proration typically based on most recent tax bill
- Supplemental tax bills are the buyer's responsibility (Civil Code 1102.6c)

## Transfer Tax

### County Transfer Tax
- **Rate:** $1.10 per $1,000 of value (or $0.55 per $500)
- **Authority:** Revenue and Taxation Code 11901-11935
- **Who pays:** Typically the seller (by custom in most areas), but negotiable
- **Exemptions:** Certain transfers are exempt (interspousal, government, foreclosure)

### City Transfer Tax
- Some cities impose additional transfer taxes
- Common examples:
  - Los Angeles: $4.50 per $1,000 (+ "Measure ULA" at higher tiers)
  - San Francisco: tiered rates from $2.50 to $30.00 per $1,000
  - Oakland: tiered rates
  - Berkeley, Culver City, Santa Monica: various rates
- Must check local ordinances for each transaction

## Title Insurance

### Types of Policies
1. **Owner's Policy (CLTA)** -- protects buyer against title defects; typically paid by seller (Southern CA) or buyer (Northern CA)
2. **Lender's Policy (ALTA)** -- protects lender; required by most lenders; paid by buyer
3. **Extended Coverage (ALTA Homeowner's)** -- broader coverage including survey matters, encroachments, etc.

### What Title Insurance Covers
- Undisclosed liens and encumbrances
- Forged documents in the chain of title
- Errors in public records
- Unknown heirs or missing parties in previous transfers
- Defective acknowledgments
- Title defects not shown in public records (extended coverage)

## FIRPTA and California Withholding

### Federal Withholding (FIRPTA -- IRC 1445)
When seller is a foreign person:

| Sale Price | Withholding Rate |
|---|---|
| Under $300,000 (buyer's residence) | 0% |
| $300,001 - $1,000,000 | 10% of sale price |
| Over $1,000,000 | 15% of sale price |

- Escrow holder withholds and remits to IRS within 20 days of closing
- Seller must file FIRPTA tax return to claim any excess withholding

### California Withholding (R&TC 18662)
- **Rate:** 3 1/3% of total sale price
- **Who:** Applied to sales by individuals, trusts, or estates who do not qualify for an exemption
- **Exemptions:**
  - Seller's principal residence (last 2 of 5 years)
  - Sale price $100,000 or less
  - Seller certifies California residency (and other conditions)
  - IRC 1031 exchange (like-kind exchange)
- **Form:** California Form 593 (completed in escrow)
- **Remittance:** Escrow withholds and remits to Franchise Tax Board

## Wire Transfer and Fund Security

### Wire Fraud Prevention
- Wire fraud is a significant risk in real estate closings
- Escrow companies must verify wire instructions directly with known contacts
- Buyers should verify wiring instructions by phone (using independently confirmed numbers)
- C.A.R. Wire Fraud Advisory (WFA) form warns parties of wire fraud risks

### Good Funds Requirements
- California requires "good funds" for closing -- funds must be collected and available
- Wire transfers are same-day funds
- Cashier's checks are typically considered good funds
- Personal checks may require hold periods

## Escrow Holder's Duties and Liabilities

### Fiduciary Duties
- Follow written escrow instructions exactly
- Maintain neutrality between parties
- Keep funds in trust accounts, properly segregated
- Account for all funds received and disbursed
- Not disburse funds without authorization from all parties (or court order)

### Liability
- Escrow holder is liable for failure to follow instructions
- Escrow holder is NOT liable for:
  - Validity of instruments deposited
  - Performance of conditions by parties
  - Acts beyond the scope of instructions

### Dispute Resolution
- If parties dispute, escrow may:
  - Hold funds pending resolution
  - File an interpleader action (court deposits the funds for judicial determination)
  - Follow cancellation instructions if both parties agree

## Timeline -- Typical California Escrow

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Day 0 | Offer accepted; escrow opened |
| Day 1-3 | Earnest money deposit delivered to escrow |
| Day 1-7 | Preliminary title report issued |
| Day 1-17 | Inspections conducted; disclosures delivered |
| Day 17 | Inspection contingency expires (C.A.R. standard) |
| Day 21 | Loan contingency expires (C.A.R. standard) |
| Day 25-27 | Loan documents drawn and delivered to escrow |
| Day 27-29 | Buyer signs loan documents; funds wired to escrow |
| Day 28-30 | Lender funds loan; documents recorded |
| Day 30 | Close of escrow; keys delivered |

(Timeline assumes 30-day escrow -- actual timelines vary)

## Penalties for Violations

| Violation | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Operating escrow without license | Criminal penalties (Financial Code 17414) |
| Trust fund violations | DFPI enforcement; license revocation; criminal penalties |
| Failure to follow instructions | Civil liability for damages |
| FIRPTA non-compliance | Buyer liability for tax withholding |
| Transfer tax non-payment | Penalties and interest; lien on property |

## Contract Builder Implementation Notes

### Escrow Integration Points
1. **Opening escrow** -- transmit purchase agreement terms to escrow
2. **Earnest money tracking** -- verify deposit receipt within contract deadline
3. **Document tracking** -- track delivery of all required documents to escrow
4. **Proration calculator** -- calculate tax and HOA prorations based on close date
5. **Transfer tax calculator** -- calculate county and city transfer taxes
6. **Withholding calculator** -- determine FIRPTA and California withholding requirements
7. **Closing statement verification** -- compare closing statement to contract terms
8. **Commission disbursement** -- calculate and verify commission allocations

### Required Data
- Escrow company name and contact
- Escrow number
- Close of escrow date
- Property tax amounts (for proration)
- HOA dues (for proration)
- Existing loan payoff amounts
- Transfer tax rates (county and city)
- Seller residency/FIRPTA status
- Vesting instructions for buyer

### Validation Rules
- Verify escrow holder is licensed or exempt
- Confirm earnest money deposit deadline compliance
- Calculate and display proration estimates
- Flag FIRPTA withholding if seller is foreign
- Flag California withholding unless exemption certified
- Verify all contingencies removed before authorizing close
