# Statute of Frauds -- Written Contract Requirements

> **Primary Authority:** California Civil Code Section 1624
> **Related Statutes:** Code of Civil Procedure Section 1971; Civil Code 1091 (conveyance in writing)
> **Scope:** All real estate contracts and agreements

---

## Overview

California's Statute of Frauds requires that certain contracts -- including those involving the sale or transfer of interests in real property -- must be in writing and signed by the party to be charged in order to be enforceable. This is the foundational legal requirement underlying all California real estate transactions.

---

## Statutory Text -- Civil Code 1624(a)

The following contracts are invalid unless they or some note or memorandum thereof are in writing and subscribed by the party to be charged or by the party's agent:

**(3)** An agreement for the leasing for a longer period than one year, or for the sale of real property, or of an interest therein; such agreement, if made by an agent of the party sought to be charged, is invalid, unless the authority of the agent is in writing, subscribed by the party sought to be charged.

**(4)** An agreement authorizing or employing an agent, broker, or any other person to purchase or sell real estate, or to lease real estate for a longer period than one year, for compensation or a commission.

## What Must Be in Writing

### Real Estate Transactions Requiring Written Agreements

| Transaction Type | Writing Required? | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Sale of real property | Yes | CC 1624(a)(3) |
| Purchase of real property | Yes | CC 1624(a)(3) |
| Lease for more than 1 year | Yes | CC 1624(a)(3) |
| Listing agreement (for commission) | Yes | CC 1624(a)(4) |
| Buyer representation agreement | Yes | CC 1624(a)(4); AB 2992 |
| Option to purchase | Yes | CC 1624(a)(3) |
| Real property sales contract (installment) | Yes | CC 1624(a)(3) |
| Easement creation | Yes | CC 1624(a)(3) |
| Deed of trust / mortgage | Yes | CC 1624(a)(3) |
| Lease for 1 year or less | No (but recommended) | CC 1624 |
| Oral modification of written contract | Generally no, must be written | CC 1698 |

## Essential Terms Required

For a written agreement to satisfy the Statute of Frauds, it must state with **reasonable certainty:**

### 1. Identity of the Parties
- Full legal names of all parties to the contract
- Entity type if applicable (corporation, LLC, trust, etc.)
- Capacity in which each party acts (individually, as trustee, etc.)

### 2. Subject Property
- Must sufficiently identify the property
- Acceptable identification methods:
  - Street address
  - Assessor's Parcel Number (APN)
  - Legal description (metes and bounds, lot and block, or government survey)
  - Any description sufficient for a reasonable person to identify the specific property

### 3. Terms and Conditions
- Purchase price (or formula for determining it)
- Material terms of the transaction
- Consideration (what each party gives and receives)

### 4. Signatures
- Must be signed (subscribed) by the **party to be charged** -- i.e., the party against whom enforcement is sought
- Both parties need not sign for the contract to be enforceable against the signing party
- Agent signature is valid only if the agent has **written authorization** from the principal

---

## Signature Requirements

### Who Must Sign
- The party to be charged (the party against whom enforcement is sought)
- If an agent signs on behalf of a principal, the agent must have **written** authority (CC 1624(a)(3))

### Types of Valid Signatures
- Handwritten signature
- Electronic signature (under UETA -- Civil Code 1633.1 et seq.)
- Initials (where customary and intended as signature)
- Agent's signature with written authorization

### Electronic Signatures
Under the Uniform Electronic Transactions Act (UETA) -- Civil Code 1633.1 et seq.:
- Electronic signatures are legally equivalent to handwritten signatures
- Both parties must consent to electronic transactions
- Electronic records must be capable of retention and accurate reproduction
- Common platforms: DocuSign, Dotloop, SkySlope, zipForms

## Multiple Writings Doctrine

The Statute of Frauds does NOT require a single document. Multiple writings may together satisfy the statute if:
- They clearly **relate to the same transaction** or subject matter
- Read together, they contain all essential terms
- At least one writing is signed by the party to be charged
- The connection between writings is established by reference, physical attachment, or other evidence

### Examples
- A signed letter referencing an unsigned property description
- An email chain where one email is signed and subsequent emails add terms
- An offer letter and a separate acceptance letter

---

## Exceptions to the Statute of Frauds

### 1. Part Performance
An oral agreement may be enforced if the party seeking enforcement can show:
- Acts of part performance that **unequivocally refer** to the oral agreement
- The party changed position in reliance on the oral agreement
- Examples: taking possession, making improvements, paying purchase price

### 2. Estoppel
A party may be estopped from asserting the Statute of Frauds if:
- One party made a promise (even orally)
- The other party reasonably relied on that promise
- The relying party suffered substantial injury
- Injustice can only be avoided by enforcement

### 3. Admission
If the party to be charged admits in litigation (in pleadings, testimony, or otherwise) that an oral agreement exists, the Statute of Frauds defense may be waived.

### 4. Full Performance
If both parties have fully performed the oral agreement, the Statute of Frauds is not a defense. The contract is considered executed, not executory.

## Agent Authorization

### Written Authorization Required (CC 1624(a)(3))
- If an agent signs a real estate contract on behalf of a principal, the agent must have **written authorization**
- Power of Attorney is the most common form of written authorization
- A listing agreement authorizes the broker to market the property but does NOT authorize the broker to sign a purchase agreement on behalf of the seller unless specifically stated

### Types of Authorization
1. **Power of Attorney** -- general or specific
2. **Specific written authorization** in the listing/representation agreement
3. **Written instruction** from principal authorizing specific actions

---

## Oral Modifications

### General Rule (Civil Code 1698)
- A written contract may only be modified by:
  - A written agreement signed by the parties
  - An executed oral agreement (if fully performed)
- An oral modification of a written real estate contract is generally unenforceable

### Exception: Executed Modifications
- If both parties act on an oral modification (e.g., agree to change the closing date and both proceed accordingly), the modification may be enforceable under the part performance doctrine

---

## Remedies and Consequences

### Unenforceable Contract
- An oral real estate agreement is **not void** -- it is merely **unenforceable** by the party who did not sign
- The agreement itself may still have evidentiary value
- Money paid under an unenforceable contract may be recovered (restitution)

### Waiver
- A party may waive the Statute of Frauds defense
- Waiver must be knowing and voluntary
- Filing a lawsuit without raising the defense may constitute waiver

### Statute of Limitations
- The Statute of Frauds is an **affirmative defense** -- it must be raised by the party asserting it
- If not raised in the answer to a complaint, it may be deemed waived
- There is no time limit on asserting the defense once raised

## Practical Applications

### Purchase Agreements
- Must be in writing with all essential terms
- C.A.R. RPA form satisfies the Statute of Frauds
- Counteroffers must also be in writing

### Listing Agreements
- Must be in writing for the broker to earn a commission
- An oral listing agreement cannot support a commission claim
- The listing agreement itself constitutes the required writing

### Buyer Representation Agreements
- Must be in writing (both Statute of Frauds and AB 2992)
- Without written agreement, broker cannot recover commission

### Lease Agreements
- Leases for more than 1 year must be in writing
- Month-to-month tenancies do not require writing (but recommended)
- Lease options (lease with option to purchase) must be in writing

## Contract Builder Implementation Notes

### Core Requirements
1. All generated contracts must be in written form (digital satisfies this)
2. Electronic signature capability must comply with UETA
3. All essential terms must be present and complete before execution
4. Both parties' identities must be clearly stated
5. Property must be sufficiently identified

### Validation Rules
- Verify all essential terms are present before allowing execution
- Require signature (electronic or otherwise) by at least the party to be charged
- Confirm agent authorization in writing if agent signs on behalf of principal
- Ensure modifications are captured in writing (amendment forms)
- Maintain audit trail of all document versions and signatures
