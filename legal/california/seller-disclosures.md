## Detailed Disclosure Requirements

### 1. Transfer Disclosure Statement (TDS)
- **Authority:** Civil Code 1102-1102.17
- **See:** [transfer-disclosure-statement.md](transfer-disclosure-statement.md) for full details
- **Who:** Seller and both agents
- **When:** As soon as practicable before transfer of title
- **Penalties:** Actual damages (Civil Code 1102.13)

### 2. Natural Hazard Disclosure (NHD)
- **Authority:** Civil Code 1103-1103.14
- **See:** [natural-hazard-disclosure.md](natural-hazard-disclosure.md) for full details
- **Who:** Seller and agents
- **When:** As soon as practicable before transfer of title
- **Penalties:** Actual damages (Civil Code 1103.13)

### 3. Lead-Based Paint Disclosure
- **Authority:** Residential Lead-Based Paint Hazard Reduction Act (Title X); 42 USC 4852d; 24 CFR Part 35
- **See:** [environmental-hazards.md](environmental-hazards.md) for full details
- **Who:** Seller of any residential property built before 1978
- **What:** Disclose known lead-based paint hazards; provide EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family From Lead in Your Home"
- **When:** Before buyer is obligated under contract (typically with purchase offer)
- **Buyer's Right:** 10-day opportunity to inspect for lead hazards (can be waived or modified by agreement)
- **Penalties:** Up to $19,507 per violation (adjusted for inflation); treble damages in private suits

### 4. Mello-Roos / Special Tax District
- **Authority:** Civil Code 1102.6b; Government Code 53311.5, 53340.2
- **See:** [mello-roos.md](mello-roos.md) for full details
- **Who:** Seller of property subject to Mello-Roos assessment
- **What:** Provide Notice of Special Tax
- **When:** Before transfer of title

### 5. Supplemental Property Tax Notice
- **Authority:** Civil Code 1102.6c
- **Who:** Seller (sole responsibility)
- **What:** Written notice to buyer explaining:
  - Assessor will revalue property at change of ownership
  - Buyer may receive 1-2 supplemental tax bills
  - Supplemental bills are NOT mailed to lender
  - Buyer is responsible for paying supplemental bills directly
- **Format:** Notice title must be in 14-point type or contrasting color; body in 12-point type or contrasting color
- **When:** As soon as practicable before transfer

### 6. Death on Property
- **Authority:** Civil Code 1710.2
- **Who:** Seller (owner or agent)
- **What Must Be Disclosed:**
  - Any death that occurred on the property within the **3 years** prior to the date the buyer offers to purchase
  - Applies to all causes of death (natural, accidental, suicide, homicide)
- **What Need NOT Be Disclosed:**
  - Deaths occurring more than 3 years prior (unless directly asked)
  - Death of a person with AIDS/HIV (cannot be disclosed regardless of when -- Civil Code 1710.2(c))
- **Direct Inquiry Exception:** If the buyer directly asks about deaths more than 3 years ago, the seller must answer **truthfully** -- the 3-year limitation does not protect intentional misrepresentation
- **When:** As soon as practicable (typically on TDS or SPQ)

### 7. Megan's Law (Sex Offender Database)
- **Authority:** Civil Code 2079.10a; Penal Code 290.46
- **Who:** Seller's agent (required notice); all parties advised
- **What:** Written notice that:
  - Information about the location of registered sex offenders is available from local law enforcement
  - Available online at the California Department of Justice website (meganslaw.ca.gov)
- **Standard Language:** Typically included in the purchase agreement (C.A.R. RPA includes Megan's Law notice)
- **When:** As part of the purchase agreement
- **Important:** The agent is NOT required to check the database; only to inform the buyer of its availability

### 8. Smoke Detector Compliance
- **Authority:** Health and Safety Code 13113.7, 13113.8
- **Who:** Seller
- **What:**
  - Seller must provide written statement that property has operable smoke detectors installed per current law
  - Smoke detectors required in each sleeping room, outside each sleeping area, and on each floor
  - Must be 10-year sealed battery type or hard-wired with battery backup
- **When:** At close of escrow (written statement delivered to buyer)
- **Note:** Effective July 1, 2014, all dwelling units must have smoke alarms that are listed and labeled in conformance with California State Fire Marshal standards

### 9. Carbon Monoxide Detector Compliance
- **Authority:** Health and Safety Code 17926-17926.2 (Carbon Monoxide Poisoning Prevention Act of 2010)
- **Who:** Seller/owner
- **What:**
  - CO detectors required in dwelling units with fossil fuel-burning appliances, attached garages, or fireplaces
  - Must comply with UL 2034 standards
  - Must be installed per NFPA 720 and manufacturer's instructions
- **When:** At transfer of title

### 10. Water Heater Bracing
- **Authority:** Health and Safety Code 19211; California Plumbing Code 510.5
- **Who:** Seller
- **What:**
  - Water heaters must be braced, anchored, or strapped to resist displacement during an earthquake
  - Seller must certify compliance or, if not in compliance, buyer and seller may negotiate who bears the cost
- **When:** At close of escrow

### 11. Military Ordnance Location
- **Authority:** Civil Code 1102.15
- **Who:** Seller (if aware)
- **What:** If the seller has actual knowledge that the property is located within 1 mile of a former federal or state ordnance location, this must be disclosed
- **When:** As part of TDS or supplemental disclosure

### 12. Industrial Use Disclosure
- **Authority:** Civil Code 1102.17
- **Who:** Seller (if aware)
- **What:** If the seller has actual knowledge that the property is affected by or zoned near industrial use that includes the presence of hazardous waste, mining operations, or other environmental hazards
- **When:** As part of TDS

### 13. Window Security Bars
- **Authority:** Civil Code 1102.6f
- **Who:** Seller
- **What:** If the property has window security bars and there is a bedroom window with security bars, the seller must disclose:
  - The presence of window security bars
  - Whether the bars have quick-release mechanisms that comply with building/fire codes
- **When:** As part of TDS

### 14. Property Flipping Disclosure (New -- Effective Jul 1, 2024)
- **Authority:** AB 968 (codified in Civil Code 1102.6e)
- **Who:** Seller of 1-4 unit residential property being resold within 18 months of acquisition
- **What:**
  - Disclose all repairs and renovations where cost of labor/materials exceeded $500
  - Provide name and contact information for each contractor used
  - Indicate whether permits were obtained for the work
- **When:** As part of TDS or supplemental disclosure
- **Purpose:** Protect buyers from undisclosed or substandard renovation work on flipped properties

### 15. Earthquake Safety Disclosure
- **Authority:** Government Code 10149.1; Civil Code 1103
- **Who:** Seller (for pre-1960 conventional wood-frame construction)
- **What:** Provide the "Homeowner's Guide to Earthquake Safety" booklet to buyer
- **When:** As soon as practicable before transfer
- **Note:** The booklet covers wood-frame home earthquake weaknesses, retrofit recommendations, and geological hazards

### 16. Environmental Hazards Booklet
- **Authority:** Health and Safety Code 25249.5 et seq. (Proposition 65)
- **Who:** Seller
- **What:** Provide the "Residential Environmental Hazards" booklet (prepared by CalEPA/CDPH)
- **When:** As soon as practicable before transfer
- **Topics Covered:** Lead, asbestos, radon, formaldehyde, mold, household hazardous waste

## Seller Disclosure Obligations by Transaction Phase

### Before Listing
- Agency Disclosure (to seller, before listing agreement)

### With or Before Purchase Offer
- Transfer Disclosure Statement (TDS)
- Natural Hazard Disclosure (NHD)
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure (pre-1978 homes)
- Agency Disclosure (to buyer)
- Seller Property Questionnaire (SPQ) -- advisory
- Statewide Buyer and Seller Advisory (SBSA) -- advisory

### During Escrow
- Mello-Roos/Special Tax Notice
- Supplemental Property Tax Notice
- HOA/CID Documents (if applicable)
- Earthquake Safety booklet
- Environmental Hazards booklet
- Property Flipping Disclosure (if applicable)

### At Close of Escrow
- Smoke Detector compliance statement
- Carbon Monoxide Detector compliance statement
- Water Heater bracing compliance statement
- FIRPTA affidavit / California withholding certificate

## Special Situations

### "As-Is" Sales
- "As-is" clauses do NOT eliminate the duty to disclose
- Seller must still complete TDS, NHD, and all other required disclosures
- "As-is" only means the seller is not agreeing to make repairs

### Estate/Trust Sales
- If the property is sold by a trustee or executor who has lived in the property, full disclosures are required
- If the trustee/executor has never lived in the property, they may complete the TDS to the best of their knowledge and indicate areas of uncertainty
- Some estate sales may qualify for TDS exemption under Civil Code 1102.2

### REO/Bank-Owned Sales
- Exempt from TDS (lender acquired through foreclosure)
- Still subject to common law duty to disclose known material defects
- Federal lead-based paint disclosure still applies to pre-1978 properties

### Foreign Seller
- FIRPTA withholding (federal): 15% of sale price withheld (with tiered exceptions)
- California withholding: 3 1/3% of sale price
- Seller must provide affidavit of non-foreign status to avoid withholding

## Penalties Summary

| Violation | Consequence |
|---|---|
| TDS failure/fraud | Actual damages (Civil Code 1102.13) |
| NHD failure/fraud | Actual damages (Civil Code 1103.13) |
| Lead paint non-disclosure | Up to $19,507/violation; treble damages |
| Death non-disclosure (within 3 yrs) | Actual damages; potential rescission |
| Failure to disclose known defects | Actual damages; fraud damages; potential punitive damages |
| Agent failure to inspect | Actual damages; DRE discipline |
| DRE license violations | License suspension/revocation; fines |

## Contract Builder Implementation Notes

### Disclosure Package Generator
The system should generate a complete disclosure package based on:
1. **Property characteristics** -- year built (lead paint trigger), HOA status, hazard zones
2. **Transaction type** -- standard sale, estate sale, REO, flip
3. **Seller type** -- individual, trust, estate, corporation, foreign seller
4. **Property history** -- renovation within 18 months (flip disclosure trigger)

### Tracking Requirements
- Track delivery dates for each disclosure
- Calculate rescission windows
- Flag missing or incomplete disclosures
- Track amendment dates and new rescission windows
- Generate compliance checklist for agents
