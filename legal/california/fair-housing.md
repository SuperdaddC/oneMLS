# Fair Housing -- California Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA)

> **Primary Authority:** Government Code 12900-12996 (Fair Employment and Housing Act)
> **Federal Counterpart:** Fair Housing Act of 1968, as amended (42 USC 3601-3619)
> **Enforcing Agency:** California Civil Rights Department (CRD), formerly Department of Fair Employment and Housing (DFEH)
> **Related:** Unruh Civil Rights Act (Civil Code 51); Civil Code 53 (discriminatory covenants)

---

## Overview

The California Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA) prohibits discrimination in housing and real estate transactions based on membership in protected classes. FEHA provides broader protections than the federal Fair Housing Act, covering additional protected categories.

---

## Protected Classes

### Federal Fair Housing Act (7 Protected Classes)
1. Race
2. Color
3. Religion
4. Sex
5. National origin
6. Familial status (families with children under 18)
7. Disability (physical or mental)

### California FEHA -- Additional Protected Classes
California FEHA adds the following, resulting in more than a dozen protected categories:

8. Sexual orientation
9. Gender identity / gender expression
10. Marital status
11. Ancestry
12. Source of income (including Section 8 vouchers)
13. Genetic information
14. Age (for certain purposes)
15. Citizenship / immigration status
16. Primary language
17. Military/veteran status

### Local Protections
Many California cities and counties provide additional protections, such as:
- Arbitrary discrimination (San Francisco)
- Weight/height (San Francisco, Santa Cruz)
- Political affiliation (various jurisdictions)
- Student status (various jurisdictions)

## Prohibited Conduct in Real Estate

### Sales Transactions
It is unlawful to discriminate in any aspect of selling residential real property, including:
1. **Refusal to sell** -- refusing to negotiate or sell based on protected class
2. **Terms and conditions** -- offering different terms, prices, or conditions based on protected class
3. **Steering** -- directing buyers toward or away from neighborhoods based on protected class
4. **Advertising** -- advertising that indicates preference or limitation based on protected class
5. **Blockbusting** -- inducing sales by representing that members of a protected class are moving into a neighborhood
6. **Discriminatory lending** -- denying or varying mortgage terms based on protected class
7. **Appraisal discrimination** -- providing biased appraisals based on neighborhood demographics

### Rental Transactions
- Same prohibitions apply to rental housing
- Source of income discrimination specifically prohibited (must accept Section 8 vouchers)

### Brokerage Services
- Denial of access to MLS or brokerage services based on protected class
- Discrimination in cooperative agreements between brokers
- Denial of membership in REALTOR associations based on protected class

## Agent and Broker Obligations

### Affirmative Duties
1. **Equal service** -- provide the same level of service to all clients regardless of protected class
2. **Fair advertising** -- ensure marketing does not indicate discriminatory preferences
3. **Equal access** -- show properties to all qualified buyers without regard to protected class
4. **No steering** -- do not direct clients to or away from neighborhoods based on demographics
5. **Training** -- maintain familiarity with fair housing laws (DRE license renewal requires fair housing education)

### Prohibited Actions by Agents
- Asking about or considering protected characteristics in qualifying buyers
- Providing different property options to clients based on protected class
- Making assumptions about neighborhood preferences based on protected class
- Refusing to present an offer based on buyer's protected class
- Commenting on neighborhood demographics to influence buyer decisions

### DRE Fair Housing Requirements
- All licensees must complete fair housing education for license renewal
- DRE may take disciplinary action for fair housing violations
- BPC 10177(l): Violation of any fair housing law is grounds for license discipline

## Reasonable Accommodations and Modifications (Disability)

### Reasonable Accommodations
- Landlords and HOAs must provide reasonable accommodations in rules, policies, and practices for persons with disabilities
- Examples: reserved parking spaces, service animal exceptions, modification of move-in procedures

### Reasonable Modifications
- Tenants may make reasonable modifications to dwelling units at their own expense
- Landlords cannot refuse reasonable modifications necessary for equal use
- For sales: seller cannot refuse to sell based on buyer's need for modifications

### Key Cases and Applications
- Service animals and emotional support animals must be accommodated
- Accessibility modifications must be permitted (e.g., grab bars, ramps)
- Reasonable accommodations in common areas may be required

## Restrictive Covenants (Civil Code 53)

### History
- Many older California properties contain CC&Rs with racially restrictive covenants
- These covenants are **void and unenforceable** under federal and California law

### Disclosure and Removal (Civil Code 12956.1, 12956.2)
- Any person who holds an ownership interest may request removal of discriminatory language from recorded documents
- The county recorder must modify the document to remove the illegal restriction
- Disclosure: If discriminatory covenants are found in CC&Rs, the document should note they are void and unenforceable

### Modification Fee
- No fee may be charged for the recording of a modification document that removes discriminatory language

## Source of Income Discrimination (Government Code 12955(p))

### Prohibition
California specifically prohibits discrimination based on source of income, including:
- Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers
- Government rental assistance
- Social Security, disability, or veteran's benefits
- Child support or alimony
- Any lawful source of income

### Landlord Obligations
- Cannot refuse to rent to a tenant because they use a housing voucher
- Cannot advertise "no Section 8" or similar language
- Must participate in the voucher process if the tenant is otherwise qualified

### Limitations
- Landlords are not required to accept below-market rents
- Market-rate rents may still apply
- Normal qualifying criteria (credit, rental history) may still be used if applied equally

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Administrative Enforcement (CRD)
- Complaints filed with the California Civil Rights Department (CRD)
- CRD investigates and may issue an accusation
- Administrative hearing before the Fair Employment and Housing Council
- Remedies include:
  - Actual damages
  - Injunctive relief
  - Civil penalties (see below)
  - Attorney fees and costs

### Civil Penalties

| Violation | Maximum Penalty |
|---|---|
| First violation | $42,500 |
| Subsequent violations | $127,500 |
| Pattern or practice | Additional damages |

### Private Lawsuits
- Individuals may file private lawsuits in Superior Court
- Available remedies:
  - Actual damages (including emotional distress)
  - Punitive damages
  - Injunctive relief
  - Attorney fees
  - Costs

### DRE Discipline
- License suspension or revocation
- Fines
- Required fair housing education
- Public disclosure of disciplinary action

### Statute of Limitations
- **CRD complaint:** 1 year from the alleged discriminatory act
- **Private lawsuit:** 2 years from the alleged discriminatory act
- **Continuing violation doctrine:** If discrimination is ongoing, the statute runs from the last discriminatory act

## Fair Housing in Real Estate Advertising

### Prohibited Language
- "No children" or "adults only" (familial status)
- "Christian neighborhood" or religious references (religion)
- "English speakers only" (national origin/primary language)
- "No Section 8" (source of income)
- "Traditional family neighborhood" (various protections)
- References to specific ethnicities or nationalities as neighborhood characteristics

### Required Advertising Elements
- **Equal Housing Opportunity** logo or statement should appear in all advertising
- HUD guidelines on fair housing advertising apply
- MLS listings must comply with fair housing requirements

## Contract Builder Implementation Notes

### Compliance Requirements
1. **No discriminatory fields** -- do not include any fields that capture or filter by protected class characteristics
2. **Equal access** -- ensure all listings are accessible to all users regardless of protected class
3. **No steering features** -- do not build recommendation algorithms that direct users based on demographics
4. **Advertising compliance** -- all listing descriptions should be reviewed for discriminatory language
5. **Equal Housing logo** -- include Equal Housing Opportunity statement/logo in platform
6. **Source of income** -- do not allow filtering or screening by source of income in rental listings

### Documentation
- Track agent fair housing education compliance
- Maintain records of equal service provision
- Document advertising review processes
