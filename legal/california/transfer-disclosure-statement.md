# Transfer Disclosure Statement (TDS)

> **Primary Authority:** California Civil Code Sections 1102 - 1102.17
> **Article:** Article 1.5 -- Disclosures Upon Transfer of Residential Property
> **C.A.R. Form:** TDS (Transfer Disclosure Statement)

---

## Overview

The Transfer Disclosure Statement is the most important disclosure document in California residential real estate transactions. It requires sellers to disclose material facts about the condition of the property, and requires agents to disclose observations from their own visual inspections.

---

## Applicability

### Transactions Covered (Civil Code 1102)
The TDS is required for any transfer by:
- Sale
- Exchange
- Real property sales contract
- Lease with an option to purchase
- Any other option to purchase
- Ground lease coupled with improvements

### Property Types Covered
- Residential real property of **one to four dwelling units**
- Includes single-family homes, duplexes, triplexes, and fourplexes

### Waiver Prohibition
Any waiver of the requirements of Article 1.5 is **void as against public policy** (Civil Code 1102).

---

## Exemptions (Civil Code 1102.2)

The following transfers are **exempt** from TDS requirements:

1. **Court-ordered transfers** -- sales pursuant to court order (including probate sales under Probate Code 10000)
2. **Foreclosure sales** -- transfers by foreclosure (trustee's sale or judicial foreclosure)
3. **Writ of execution** -- sales under a writ of execution
4. **Eminent domain** -- transfers by condemnation
5. **Bankruptcy trustee** -- transfers by a fiduciary in the administration of a decedent's estate, guardianship, conservatorship, or trust
6. **Government transfers** -- transfers by a state or local government entity
7. **REO sales** -- transfers from a lender who acquired the property through foreclosure (Note: the exemption applies only to the lender; if the lender is also the original owner, the exemption may not apply)
8. **Co-owner transfers** -- transfers between co-owners
9. **Spouse/domestic partner** -- transfers to a spouse or domestic partner per dissolution decree
10. **New construction** -- transfers of newly constructed property that has never been occupied (builder warranty applies instead)

**Important:** Even when exempt from the TDS, sellers still have a common law duty to disclose known material defects.

---

## TDS Form Content (Civil Code 1102.3, 1102.6, 1102.6a)

The TDS form has three main sections:

### Section I -- Seller's Information
The seller must disclose information about:

**Structural/Systems:**
- Appliances included in the sale
- Roof type, age, and condition
- Electrical, plumbing, and sewer systems
- Water supply and water heater
- Heating and air conditioning systems
- Pool/spa equipment
- Garage door opener(s)

**Property Conditions:**
- Foundation issues, settling, slipping, sliding
- Interior/exterior walls, ceilings, floors
- Windows, doors, and screens
- Insulation
- Drainage issues
- Fences, retaining walls, driveways, sidewalks
- Fireplace(s) and chimney condition

**Environmental/Hazard:**
- Substances, materials, or products that may be an environmental hazard (asbestos, formaldehyde, radon, lead paint, fuel tanks, contaminated soil/water)
- Features shared with adjoining properties (walls, fences, driveways)
- Any encroachments, easements, or similar matters
- Room additions, structural modifications, or repairs made without permits
- Room additions, structural modifications, or repairs not in compliance with building codes
- Fill (compacted or otherwise) on the property or any portion thereof
- Any settling from any cause, or slippage, sliding, or other soil problems
- Flooding or drainage problems
- Major damage from fire, earthquake, floods, or landslides
- Zoning violations, nonconforming uses, violations of setback requirements
- Neighborhood noise problems or other nuisances
- CC&Rs or other deed restrictions or obligations
- Homeowners Association requirements
- Any lawsuits against the seller affecting or relating to the property
- Any common areas (pools, walkways, common walls, recreation areas)
- Any notices from government agencies or insurance companies regarding defects
- Any deaths on the property within the last 3 years

### Section II -- Agent's Inspection Disclosure
Each agent (listing and selling) must conduct a **reasonably competent and diligent visual inspection** of the accessible areas and disclose findings.

**Listing Agent Section:**
- Agent must inspect the property and report observable conditions
- Based on a visual inspection per Civil Code 2079

**Selling (Buyer's) Agent Section:**
- Buyer's agent must also conduct and report visual inspection findings

### Section III -- Agent's Inspection (continued)
- Additional observations and material facts
- Explanations of any "Yes" answers from seller or agents

---

## Delivery Requirements (Civil Code 1102.3)

### Timing
- Must be delivered **as soon as practicable before transfer of title**
- Should be delivered before or concurrent with the purchase offer when possible

### Methods of Delivery
- In person
- By mail
- By electronic delivery

### Rescission Rights After Late Delivery
If the TDS (or any material amendment) is delivered **after** execution of the purchase offer:

| Delivery Method | Rescission Period |
|---|---|
| In person | 3 calendar days |
| By mail | 5 calendar days |
| Electronic delivery | 5 calendar days |

The buyer must deliver written notice of rescission to the seller or seller's agent.

---

## Seller's Duty of Good Faith (Civil Code 1102.7)

The TDS must be completed in **"good faith"**, which is expressly defined as **"honesty in fact in the conduct of the transaction"** (Civil Code 1102.7).

Key points:
- The seller is not required to have expertise in construction, engineering, or other technical areas
- The seller IS required to disclose known material facts
- "As-is" clauses do NOT eliminate the duty to disclose
- The duty continues throughout the transaction -- if new facts arise, the seller must amend the TDS

---

## Agent's Duties

### Visual Inspection Duty (Civil Code 2079)
Both the listing agent and buyer's agent must:
1. Conduct a **reasonably competent and diligent visual inspection** of accessible areas
2. Disclose all facts materially affecting value or desirability revealed by the inspection
3. Complete their respective sections of the TDS

### Standard of Care (Civil Code 2079.2)
The standard is measured by the degree of knowledge required to obtain a real estate license. Agents are NOT required to:
- Inspect inaccessible areas
- Inspect off-site areas
- Research public records or permits
- Have expertise in structural engineering, geology, or other technical fields

### Agent Visual Inspection Disclosure (C.A.R. Form AVID)
While the TDS includes agent inspection sections, many agents also complete a separate AVID form to document their visual inspection findings.

---

## Amendments and Updates (Civil Code 1102.3(d))

If the seller or agent discovers new material information after the TDS has been delivered:
- An **amended TDS** must be delivered to the buyer
- The buyer gets a new rescission period (3 or 5 days depending on delivery method)
- Amendments must be in writing

---

## Liability (Civil Code 1102.13)

Any person who **willfully or negligently** violates or fails to perform any duty prescribed by Article 1.5 shall be liable for:
- **Actual damages** suffered by the buyer/transferee
- No provision for statutory damages (only actual damages)
- Potential liability extends to agents who fail to conduct visual inspection

### Limitation on Liability (Civil Code 1102.4)
- Neither the transferor nor the agent is required to disclose conditions of which they are personally not aware
- Agents are protected from liability for errors/omissions made by the seller in good faith that the agent had no reason to question

### Statute of Limitations
- **Two years** from discovery of the breach, or
- **Two years** from when the breach should have been discovered through reasonable diligence

---

## Interaction with Other Disclosures

The TDS works in conjunction with (but does not replace):
- Natural Hazard Disclosure Statement (Civil Code 1103)
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure (federal requirement for pre-1978 homes)
- Mello-Roos/Special Tax Disclosure
- HOA/CID Disclosures
- Supplemental Property Tax Disclosure
- Smoke Detector/CO Detector/Water Heater Compliance

## Contract Builder Implementation Notes

### Required Fields for TDS Generation
1. Property address and APN
2. Property type (single-family, duplex, etc.)
3. Year built
4. Seller identity and ownership history
5. All applicable system/structural conditions (from seller questionnaire)
6. Agent inspection findings (both listing and buyer's agent)

### Validation Rules
- Verify property is 1-4 residential units (otherwise TDS not required)
- Check for exemptions (probate, foreclosure, REO, etc.)
- Track delivery date for rescission window calculation
- Flag if delivered after offer execution (triggers rescission rights)
- Require amendment tracking for post-delivery changes

### Digital Signature Requirements
- Seller signature and date on Section I
- Listing agent signature and date on Section II
- Buyer's agent signature and date on Section II/III
- Buyer acknowledgment of receipt with date
